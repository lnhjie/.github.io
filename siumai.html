Siu Mai (燒賣)

Fillings are of course customizable to your likings!

Catonese Dim Sum Dumplings
90 minutes
INTERMEDIATE
6 SERVINGS

Introduction

Siu mai also known as a pork and shrimp dumpling is a classic cantonese dim sum dish, that is loved and appreciated world wide. The traditional way is to fill it with minced pork and shrimps.

Ingredients

  • ♥ 450g Pork, ground or hand-minced
  • ♥ 110g Shrimp, cut in small pieces & de-veined
  • ♥ 1 package Dumpling wrappers
  • ♥ 1 Egg
  • ♥ 1 tsp Sesame oil
  • ♥ 1 tbsp Oil
  • ♥ 1 tbsp Cornstarch
  • ♥ ½ tbsp Oyster sauce
  • ♥ ½ tsp Chicken bouillon
  • ♥ ½ tsp Salt
  • ♥ ½ tbsp Ginger
  • ♥ 2 tbsp Soy sauce
  • ♥ 2 tbsp Water
  • Optional: 1 tbsp Pork Belly Fat

Step-by-Step

  1. To make the pork filling, mix 1 pound ground pork, ½ tablespoonsof ginger, 1 egg, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, ½ teaspoon salt, ½ tablespoon oyster sauce, 1 tablespoons oil, 1 tablespoon corn starch, and ½ teaspoon chicken bouillon. Mix in clockwise direction only to help with myosin development. This helps keep the pork tender.
  2. After pork mixture is stirred well, add ¼ pound shrimp. If you have large shrimp, cut in small pieces.
  3. If you have pork belly on hand, mince the fat and add 1 tablespoons of it into the pork filling mixture. This makes the pork super juicy! This is optional though because I understand that not everyone has pork belly fat on hand.
  4. Then, add 2 tablespoons water and 1 teaspoon sesame oil and make sure the pork mixture is mixed well.
  5. Next, you want to get your dumpling wrappers ready. Most of them come in a square shape so you should cut them in circle. If you can find wrappers that are already in circle shape, then you can skip this step. An easy trick is to cut one wrapper in a good circle and lay it on top of a stack of wrappers to get all of them an even size.
  6. To wrap the wrappers, you need a little bit of water in a bowl. Paint the edge of the wrapper with the water. This helps the wrapper stick together.
  7. Add 1 tbs (and a little more, depending on size of the wrapper) of the pork filling in the center and scrunch the edges.
  8. Place the siu mai on a flat surface and make it stand tall.
  9. After wrapping all the siu mai, you can steam them! I steamed around 10 siu mai in med-high heat for 12-15 minutes and they’re ready to eat!
